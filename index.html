The 4 agents

Every Agent, Every Prompt

Each agent has one job. The prompts below are the exact ones running in my Cowork setup. Copy them directly, swap in your tools and topics, and you're done.

1
The Story Scanner
Runs Monday at 7am, reads last 7 days of meeting notes

Scans all your Granola meeting notes from the past week and extracts build-in-public story candidates. Not summaries of what happened. Actual post ideas framed as stories, with the hook angle already identified.

The output is a list of 5 candidates ranked by "would someone outside the company care about this?" The agents don't ask you to decide what's interesting. They've already filtered for that.

granola
notion
Exact prompt
Read all my Granola meeting notes from the past 7 days. For each meeting, identify any moment that could become a build-in-public LinkedIn post: a decision we made and why, something we learned the hard way, a counterintuitive result, a milestone, or a problem we solved in an unexpected way. Output a numbered list of 5 story candidates. For each one: (1) the core event in one sentence, (2) why an outsider would find it interesting, (3) a suggested hook opener (first line of the post). Rank them from most to least compelling. Save this list to a new Notion page titled "Content Candidates — [this week's date]".
2
The Trend Researcher
Runs after Agent 1, drops a brief in Notion

Researches what's trending in your specific content categories and delivers a structured brief with angles, data points, and what the conversation is missing. You stop showing up to trending topics with nothing to add.

The key is telling it exactly which topics matter to you. Generic "trending in AI" research is noise. Research scoped to your actual content pillars is signal.

web search
notion
Exact prompt
Research what's trending this week across these three topics: (1) AI tools and automation for founders and operators, (2) employee advocacy and LinkedIn content strategy, (3) build-in-public founder content. For each topic: find 2-3 posts or articles getting significant engagement this week, identify the angle that's getting traction, note what's being left out of the conversation (the gap I could fill), and suggest one specific post idea that adds something new rather than repeating what's already been said. Format as a weekly trend brief and save it to Notion, titled "Trend Brief — [this week's date]". Keep it scannable. I'll read it in 5 minutes.
3
The Hook Drafter
Reads Agents 1 and 2 output, writes 5 full drafts

Takes the story candidates and the trend brief and writes complete post drafts in your voice. Not just hooks. Full posts, ready to pick and refine. The difference between this and generic AI content is that it's working from your actual material, not from thin air.

The voice instructions are the most important part of this prompt. Spend time on them once, and the output quality improves dramatically across every post it writes.

notion
Exact prompt
Read the Content Candidates and Trend Brief pages I saved to Notion this week. Pick the 3 strongest post opportunities, combining my personal stories with the trending angles where they fit naturally. Write a complete first-draft LinkedIn post for each one. Voice instructions: first person, direct and conversational, no corporate language, lead with something surprising or counterintuitive, short punchy lines, end with a question or a reframe that invites a reply. Under 250 words per post. No em dashes. Save all 3 drafts to a single Notion page titled "Post Drafts — [this week's date]". Label each draft clearly so I can pick one and move it to scheduled.
4
The Calendar Builder
Populates your content calendar automatically

Takes the approved drafts and populates your content calendar in Notion with suggested posting dates based on topic mix, cadence, and what performed well previously. You don't schedule anything manually.

This is the last step in the chain and the one that makes the whole system feel real. When you open Monday with a populated calendar rather than a blank one, the whole relationship with content shifts.

notion
Exact prompt
Read my Post Drafts page for this week in Notion. I have [X] posts ready. Add each one to my Notion content calendar database with: post draft (full text), suggested publish date (spread across the week, Tuesday through Friday, skip Monday), status set to "Ready to Schedule", and a one-line note on why you chose that date for that post (e.g. "trending topic, catch early week momentum"). If there are fewer than 3 posts, flag which gaps exist and suggest what type of content would round out the week based on what I've posted in the last 30 days.
Getting started

How to Set This Up in Under an Hour

This is not a complex system. It took me longer than an hour the first time because I was figuring it out. You have the playbook. Here's the exact sequence.

1

Connect Granola and Notion to Cowork

In Claude Desktop, open Settings and connect Granola and Notion as MCP tools. Test each connection with a simple read prompt before moving on. Do not skip the test step.

2

Create your Notion content infrastructure

You need three pages: a "Weekly Content" folder, a content calendar database (Name, Post Draft, Publish Date, Status), and somewhere for the agents to save their weekly output. Keep it simple.

3

Run Agent 1 first, manually, to calibrate

Before automating anything, run the Story Scanner prompt by hand on one week of Granola notes. Read the output. If the story candidates feel off, adjust the voice in the prompt. This calibration pass saves you from automating bad output.

4

Run all 4 agents in sequence and review the chain

Run each agent once manually, in order, to verify the handoff works. Agent 2 reads nothing from Agent 1, so it can run in parallel. Agents 3 and 4 depend on what came before, so run those in sequence.

5

Automate the Monday trigger

Set up a recurring Claude Cowork task to run Agents 1 and 2 every Monday at 7am. Agents 3 and 4 can run when you're ready to review, or you can chain them to fire automatically after Agent 2 completes.
